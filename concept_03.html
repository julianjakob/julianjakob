THEORETICAL OVERVIEW

when people watch a movie, they connect with and experience the content on an emotional level. different scenes or acts evoke various emotions. shapes derived from basic forms such as triangles, circles, or squares should embody these emotions. the diversity of forms represents the emotional spectrum of the film and emphasizes the central role of emotions in the art of film. each contour and expression tells its own story, connecting the viewer directly to the essence of the film and emphasizing the indispensable role of emotions in the film experience.
